Strategic Recommendations

Decision framework for NeoWallet based on verified evidence

1. Strategic Situation

NeoWallet has a clear vision (social banking for Gen Z) but faces three structural problems that the evidence base has surfaced:

  1. The core differentiator is the core risk. Public finance feeds — the thing that makes NeoWallet different — is exactly what users resist. [IG-01] vs [IG-07]
  2. The business model has no market precedent. Subscription + social has never worked in fintech. [IG-11]
  3. The tech stack creates a UX gap. Cordova + OldGuard means NeoWallet launches slower and less polished than every competitor. [IG-05], [IG-06]

None of these are dealbreakers, but they cannot be ignored. The strategy must address all three explicitly.

2. Three Strategic Paths

Based on the evidence, three viable positioning strategies emerge. Each resolves the conflicts differently:

Path A: "Private Squads" — Social by Invitation

Resolves: CF-03 (privacy vs social) by making all social features opt-in and group-based. No public feeds. Finance stays private; squads are the social unit.

Pricing: Freemium. Free core banking + $2-3/mo for squad features (shared pots, group goals, premium profiles).

Differentiation: CashFlow Z is "finance Twitter" (everything public). NeoWallet is "finance group chat" (private by default, social by choice).

Evidence: Aligns with IG-07 (privacy), IG-10 ($2-3 willingness), IG-12 (trust). Keeps IG-01 (social) but reframes it.

Risk: Smaller viral potential. No public feed means no organic discovery. Growth requires marketing spend.

Path B: "Finance for Clout" — Double Down on Social

Resolves: CF-03 by accepting that public finance is polarizing and targeting the segment that wants it (Taylor-like users, not Alex-like users).

Pricing: Free + cosmetic monetization (profile skins, metal cards, status badges). No subscription.

Differentiation: Compete directly with CashFlow Z on features, bet on squad goals as the hook.

Evidence: Aligns with IG-01 (social vision). Contradicts IG-07 (privacy). Requires accepting a smaller addressable market.

Risk: Network effects favor incumbent (CashFlow Z has 10M users). Launching with Cordova + T+2 means worse UX than the competitor you're challenging.

Path C: "Status Quo" — Launch as Proposed

Resolves: Nothing. $5 subscription + public social feeds + Cordova + OldGuard.

Evidence against: IG-07 (users find it creepy), IG-08 (won't pay), IG-10 ($5 too steep), IG-11 (no precedent), IG-12 (trust barrier).

Risk: Every external data point contradicts this path. The brief's assumptions are the only supporting evidence, and they are untested.

3. Recommendation

Path A ("Private Squads") is the strongest evidence-backed strategy. It resolves the privacy conflict, aligns with user willingness-to-pay, and creates a differentiated position between CashFlow Z (too public) and SaveMate (too serious).

5. Decision Points

After completing P0 research, the following decisions must be made by stakeholders:

  1. CEO + Product: Social positioning — private-first (Path A) or social-first (Path B)? Input: CF-03 research results
  2. CEO + Finance: Pricing model — freemium, low subscription ($2-3), or alternative monetization? Input: CF-04 benchmarks

These 3 decisions unlock the remaining 75% of the product architecture. Until they're made, only Core Banking can be fully specified.

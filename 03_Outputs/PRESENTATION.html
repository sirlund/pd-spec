This presentation summarizes the current state of the NeoWallet product strategy based on 6 source documents analyzed through PD-Spec. 2 of 4 modules are blocked on unresolved conflicts.

The Problem

Gen Z finds banking boring and solitary.

They manage money reactively, resist manual budgeting tools, and expect mobile-native, social experiences in everything — except finance.

"I usually just look at my bank balance before I buy something. If I have money, I buy it." — Alex

But "social finance" is not as straightforward as it seems. User research reveals fundamental tensions between the social vision and what users actually want.

The Vision

"Social banking" — multiplayer finance with shared pots, public feeds, and squad goals.

[IG-01] "We want to make finance viral, multiplayer, and deeply integrated with social status."

Mobile-only. If it's not in your pocket, it doesn't exist. [IG-03]

This is the brief's vision. But the next slides show why it's more complicated than it sounds.

Privacy vs Social

"I honestly find it creepy when people broadcast what they pay for. I distinctly remember my friend getting roasted for spending $50 on a game skin, and I don't want that energy."

— Alex, user interview [IG-07]

CF-03 — Needs research: Additional user interviews on privacy expectations in social finance apps.

This is the single biggest risk to the product. The core differentiator (public finance) is exactly what users resist. We need more data before deciding on positioning.

Pricing Model

The brief says $5/month. Every external source disagrees:

  • Alex: "Why would I pay just to hold my own money?" [IG-08]
  • Taylor: "$2-3 maybe. $5 feels steep." [IG-10]
  • Market: No successful app combines subscription + social. [IG-11]

CF-04 — Needs research: Freemium benchmarks, conversion rates, ARPU analysis.

3 independent sources all point the same direction. The $5 model needs serious reconsideration or very strong justification.

Decision Made: Tech Stack

CF-02 — Resolved: CEO confirmed Cordova/React wrapper for 3-month launch. Flutter migration after PMF.

The brief said "100% native." The CTO said "Cordova or we miss the deadline."

Decision: ship fast with Cordova, plan Flutter migration post-PMF. [IG-06] wins for MVP, [IG-03] remains long-term target

This is the only fully resolved conflict. CEO made the call — speed over polish for v1.

Trust Barrier

"Would you link your main bank account?"
"Hell no. Security risk. I'd only put like 'play money' in there."

— Jordan [IG-12]

Only 1 source addresses this. The brief ignores security perception entirely.

Implication: Onboarding cannot require primary bank linking.

This is an evidence gap. We have one strong signal but need more data to know if it's universal or segment-specific.

Next Steps

  1. Privacy research — User interviews on social finance expectations → unblock CF-03
  2. Pricing research — Freemium benchmarks, ARPU analysis → unblock CF-04
  3. CTO + Product sync — Stark API timeline, real-time feasibility → address CF-01
  4. Trust research — Security perception in fintech → validate IG-12
  5. CEO alignment — PMF criteria and Flutter migration trigger → operationalize CF-02
These are ordered by impact. Privacy and pricing block 2 of 4 modules — they should be prioritized.

# Source: User Interview - Alex M.

- **Type:** interview
- **Date:** 2026-02-12
- **Participants:** Alex (User), User Researcher
- **Context:** Deep dive with a college student about their spending habits.

---

**Researcher:** Tell me about how you track expenses.

**Alex:** I tried using Mint and YNAB, but they are too manual. I usually just look at my bank balance before I buy something. If I have money, I buy it.

**Researcher:** How do you feel about sharing your finances with friends?

**Alex:** Oh, absolutely not. Money is private. I have a Venmo, but I keep my transactions private. I honestly find it creepy when people broadcast what they pay for. I distinctly remember my friend getting roasted for spending $50 on a game skin, and I don't want that energy.

**Researcher:** What about AI? Would you like an AI to help you budget?

**Alex:** Maybe? But I don't want it to be annoying. If it starts lecturing me or trying to be "funny", I'll uninstall it. I just want it to quietly move money into savings without me noticing. Also, I do huge management on my laptop. I hate doing complex finance stuff on my phone screen, it's too small for spreadsheets.

**Researcher:** Would you pay for a finance app?

**Alex:** Probably not. There are so many free ones. Chime is free, CashApp is free. Why would I pay a subscription just to hold my own money?
